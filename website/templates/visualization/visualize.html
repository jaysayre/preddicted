Visualizing the Data

Our data set contains 44,000 posts posted by 20,000 authors. This means that the average successful user has approximately two submissions in the lists we investigate. The following visualization shows what subreddits have more active and successful users with multiple posts. It illustrates the fact that subreddits that tell stories like “nosleep” generally have an succesful user base whereas larger subreddits seem to be dominated by randomness and being experienced with posting there does not indicate success.

Another thing which is interesting to look at is how the posting date relates to the score. You can clearly see how the highest score can be reached within no time. one of the posts with a score of 4000 only took 6 days to get there. On the whole, it seems that popular posts become so quickly, and therefore, it doesn't happen often that an obscure post on Reddit will remain obscure for months and then suddenly become popular.

Did you ever wonder whether you should put text in a reddit post? Shouldn’t the question alone be sufficient enough for people to grasp what you are asking?

Investigating this, the answer is yes! You can see in red all posts that have no additional text and in blue the ones with text. The ones without clearly get a better score. What does this mean for you? It means that you should formulate your posts such that people can actually understand what you are asking them.
